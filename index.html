Botanical Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Phylum: Tracheophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Rosales
  • Family: Moraceae
  • Genus: Ficus
  • Species: F. benghalensis

Physical Characteristics

  • Height: 20–30 meters
  • Canopy Spread: Up to 200 m
  • Trunk Diameter: 10+ meters
  • Leaf Size: 10–20 cm
  • Leaf Shape: Ovate
  • Bark Type: Smooth, Grey
  • Root System: Aerial Prop Roots

Ecological Data

  • Lifespan: 500+ years
  • CO₂ Absorption: 60 kg/day
  • O₂ Production: 1700 kg/year
  • Shade Area: 20,000 m²
  • Water Uptake: 2000 L/day
  • Soil Binding: Excellent
  • Wildlife Support: 120+ species

Historical & Cultural Significance

Religious Importance

Hinduism:

Sacred to Lord Vishnu; symbol of eternal life and wisdom. Worshipped during Vat Purnima.

Buddhism:

Buddha attained enlightenment under a Bodhi tree (Ficus religiosa), closely related species.

Jainism:

Associated with the 3rd Tirthankara, Sambhavanatha; symbol of spiritual growth.

Cultural Heritage

National Symbol:

India's National Tree since 1950; represents unity in diversity and immortality.

Literature:

Featured in ancient texts like the Mahabharata, Ramayana, and various Puranas.

Folklore:

Village gathering place; witness to stories, marriages, and community events.

Environmental Impact & Conservation

Ecosystem Services

Air Purification:

Filters 2.5 tons of CO₂ annually; releases oxygen 24/7.

Soil Conservation:

Extensive roots prevent erosion and improve soil structure.

Water Cycle:

Transpires 2000 L/day; increases local humidity and rainfall.

Biodiversity Hub:

Supports 120+ species (birds, insects, mammals, epiphytes).

Conservation Status

Current Threats:

Urban development, pollution, climate change, fragmentation.

Protection Measures:

Heritage designation, legal protection, community programs.

Propagation:

Seeds, cuttings, air layering for conservation breeding.

Global Distribution:

Native to India; Tropical & Subtropical regions widely cultivated.
